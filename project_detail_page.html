Research

Methods

Formal Research

As mentioned before, the team members had to come to terms with the fact that they were not experts of social inclusion in society. However, they could research the work of others who held more knowledge about the topic. Formal research was the first research method carried, and it consisted in the investigation of journal articles and scholarly documents that referenced social inclusion. While useful, the team identified that the project had to work in a practical environment and thus practical research methods would end up trumping any formal research.

Interviews

Interviews were single handedly the most important research method implemented. The team learned a lot by interviewing individuals and probing them on the topic of social inclusion. This method was particularly useful because it was based on real experiences.

Surveys

While social inclusion or exclusion can't be quantified, there were some measures that the team wanted to uncover. The surveys implemented by the team were conducted as a follow up from a discovery in the interviews. The aim of the surveys wasto have scale in options and facts over certain topics. While they were not very helpful in conceptualising the idea of social inclusion, they helped narrow down possible design directions.

Observation

Noticing social inclusion through observation would not be as easy as identifying itthrough other methods. However, if there the lucky chance occurs then the benefit of observation is that the observer can identify exclusion in its natural habitat.
